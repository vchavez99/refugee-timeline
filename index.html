The Evolution of Refugee Policy

Welcome! This is a non-extensive timeline of the evolution of policy pertaining to refugees and the events that prompted them. Please refer to this repository's README file for a full list of references.

1921

  • The League of Nations establishes an international programme of material assistance for destitute Russian refugees.
  • Fridtjof Nansen is appointed as the first High Commissioner of the International Refugee Organization (IRO).

1922

  • Intergovernmental Conference on Identity Certificates for Russian Refugees results in the creation of the Nansen Passport. (July 3rd - 5th)
  • The Nobel Peace Prize is awarded to Fridtjof Nansen for his work as High Commissioner for Refugees.

1933

  • James McDonald is appointed as High Commissioner of the IRO.

1943

  • United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration is established.

1946

  • The United Kingdom establishes the European Volunteer Worker Scheme (lasts until 1947).
  • Calcutta Killings as tensions build up on the prospect of the partition of India (August 16).

1947

  • Mass violence targeted at women in Rawalpindi as tensions build up on the prospect of the partition of India (March).

1948

  • More than a million people enter West Bengal (by June).

1949

  • The United Nations Relief and Workd Agency for Palestine Refugees in the Near East is created through Resolution 302 IV (December).
  • The establishment of the People's Republic of China causes an exodus of refugees to Hong Kong.

1950

  • UNRWA starts working (May 1st).
  • The United Nations Korean Reconstruction Agency is created by the United Nations General Assembly.

1951

  • UNHCR is born through the Convention Relating to the Status of Refugees.

1956

  • Hungarian refugees become eligible for UNHCR assistance.

1957

  • The United Nations General Assembly decides that the matter of Chinese refugees in Hong Kong is a concern to the international community.

1959

  • UNHCR involvement in Algeria starts (February).

1967

  • The Protocol Relating to the Status of Refugees signed in New York.

1969

  • Organization of African Unityâ€™s Convention Governing the Specific Aspects of Refugee Problems in Africa.

1984

  • Cartagena Declaration on Refugees.

1990

  • UNHCR Position Paper on Gender-Related Persecution.

1991

  • UNHCR Guidelines on the Protection of Refugee Women.

1993

  • UNHCR Guidelines on Women Refugee Claimants Fearing Gender-Related Persecution.
  • Attorney-General of Canada and War established that persecutory actors did not have to be state actors.
  • Rape and sexual slavery in conflict are for the first time recognized as crimes against humanity in the ICTY.

1994

  • UNHCR Refugee Children Guidelines of Protection and Care.

1995

  • US Immigration and Naturalization Service issues the Considerations for Asylum Officers Adjudicating Asylum Claims for Women.

1996

  • Kasinga, 211 and N. Dec 357 becomes the first US decision to recognize female genital mutilation as a form of gender-based persecution.

1999

  • Islam v Secretary of State for the Home Department offered assylum to 2 Pakistani women who had suffered domestic violence and were at risk of being accused of adultery if returned to Pakistan.

2000

  • UN Security Council Resolution 1325 on Women, Peace and Security.

2002

  • Rome Statute of the International Criminal Court includes rape, sexual slavery, enforced prostitution, forced pregnancy, enforced sterilization, or any other comparable form of sexual violence as crimes against humanity.

2004

  • Peace-deal brokered in Sudan meant that UNHCR ceased interviewing (non-Darfuri) Sudanese asylum applicants in Cairo.

2007

  • Yogyakarta Principles on the Application of International Human Rights Law in Relation to Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity.

2008

  • UNHCR Guidance Note on Refugee Claims Relating to Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity.

2011

  • UNHCR Need to Know Guidance on working with LGBTI persons.

2012

  • UNHCR Claims to Refugee Status based on Sexual Orientation and/or Gender Identity within the context of Article 1A(2) of the 1951 Convention and/or its 1967 Protocol relating to the Status of Refugees.
